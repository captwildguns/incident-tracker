## Introduction

The Student Transportation Incident Tracker is your comprehensive tool for managing all incidents that occur during student transportation. This guide will help you navigate the system and make the most of its features.

### What Can You Do?

- **Track Incidents:** Record and monitor all transportation-related incidents
- **Manage Students:** View student profiles and their incident histories
- **Monitor Fleet:** Track vehicle status, maintenance, and incidents
- **Oversee Drivers:** Manage driver information, assignments, and safety records
- **Generate Reports:** Create custom reports and analytics
- **Communicate:** Track all communications with drivers regarding incidents

## Getting Started

### Accessing the System

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
2. Navigate to the application URL provided by your district
3. The application will load showing the Dashboard as the default view

### Navigation

The top navigation bar (omnibar) provides access to all major sections:

- **Dashboard:** Overview and statistics
- **Incidents:** Incident management
- **Students:** Student records and incident history
- **Vehicles:** Fleet management
- **Drivers:** Driver management
- **New Incident:** Create new incident reports

## Dashboard

The Dashboard is your command center for monitoring incident activity.

### Key Metrics (Top Row)

1. **Total Incidents:** Overall count of all recorded incidents
2. **Active Incidents:** Number of incidents currently open or under review
3. **Critical Incidents:** Count of high-priority incidents requiring immediate attention
4. **Avg Response Time:** Average time to respond to incidents (in hours)

### Incident Trends Chart

The line chart displays incident trends over time:
- **X-axis:** Months (January through December)
- **Y-axis:** Number of incidents
- **Hover:** View exact numbers for any data point
- **Purpose:** Identify seasonal patterns and trends

### Incidents by Type (Pie Chart)

Visual breakdown of incident categories:
- **Student Behavior:** Conduct-related incidents
- **Safety Violation:** Safety protocol breaches
- **Mechanical:** Vehicle-related issues
- **Accident:** Collision or injury incidents
- **Other:** Miscellaneous incidents

**Usage:** Click the legend to highlight specific categories

### Incidents by School (Bar Chart)

Compare incident counts across schools:
- **Color-Coded Bars:** Each school represented by district brand colors
- **Hover:** View exact incident counts
- **Purpose:** Identify schools requiring additional attention

### Recent Incidents Timeline

Shows the 5 most recent incidents with:
- **Incident ID:** Clickable link to full details
- **Type Badge:** Visual category indicator
- **Description:** Brief incident summary
- **Date:** When the incident occurred
- **Status Badge:** Current incident status

**Action:** Click "View All Incidents" to see the complete list

## Creating New Incidents

The New Incident page guides you through creating detailed incident reports.

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Basic Information

**Required Fields:**
- **Incident Date:** Calendar picker (defaults to today)
- **Incident Time:** Time picker
- **Incident Type:** Dropdown selection
  - Student Behavior
  - Safety Violation
  - Mechanical
  - Accident
  - Other
- **Severity:** Priority level
  - Critical (red)
  - High (orange)
  - Medium (yellow)
  - Low (gray)

**Optional Fields:**
- **Location:** Where the incident occurred
- **Run:** Bus run if applicable

**Validation:**
- Date cannot be in the future
- Time must be valid (HH:MM format)
- Type and Severity are required

#### Step 2: Involved Parties

**Student Information:**
- **Student Name:** Dropdown search (type to filter)
- **Student ID:** Auto-populated when name selected
- **Grade:** Auto-populated
- **School:** Auto-populated

**Driver Information:**
- **Driver Name:** Dropdown search
- **Employee ID:** Auto-populated
- **Assigned Vehicle:** Auto-populated

**Vehicle Information:**
- **Vehicle/Bus Number:** Dropdown search
- Shows: Bus name, make/model, year
- Auto-populates if driver selected first

**Add Multiple Students:**
- Click "+ Add Another Student" to include additional students
- Each student has their own row with full information

#### Step 3: Incident Details

**Description:**
- Large text area (500 character minimum)
- Describe what happened in detail
- Include timeline of events
- Character counter shows remaining characters

**Actions Taken:**
- What immediate actions were taken?
- Who was notified?
- Any first aid administered?

**Injury Information:**
- **Were there any injuries?** Radio buttons (Yes/No)
- If Yes, additional fields appear:
  - Nature of injuries
  - Medical attention required (Yes/No)
  - Treatment provided
  - Hospital transport (if applicable)

**Witnesses:**
- Click "+ Add Witness" to add witness information
- Fields per witness:
  - Name
  - Type (Student/Staff/Parent/Other)
  - Contact information
  - Brief statement

#### Step 4: Additional Information

**Attachments:**
- **Photos:** Drag and drop or click to upload
  - Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, HEIC
  - Max size: 10MB per file
  - Multiple files supported
- **Documents:** PDF, DOC, DOCX
- **Videos:** MP4, MOV (max 50MB)

**Follow-up Required:**
- Toggle switch for follow-up flag
- Assign to: Dropdown of administrators
- Due date: Calendar picker
- Priority: Urgent/Standard/Low

**Internal Notes:**
- Notes visible only to administrators
- Not included in parent communications

**Tags:**
- Custom tags for categorization
- Searchable in reports
- Examples: "First Offense", "Repeat Behavior", "Parent Notified"

#### Step 5: Review and Submit

**Review Screen:**
- All entered information displayed
- Edit buttons for each section
- Click section to return and edit
- Validation summary at top (shows any missing required fields)

**Submit Options:**
1. **Save as Draft:** Save without submitting
   - Can return and complete later
   - Found in "My Drafts"
   - No notifications sent
   
2. **Submit Incident:** Complete submission
   - Validates all required fields
   - Sends notifications to:
     - Assigned administrator
     - Driver's supervisor
     - Safety coordinator (if Critical/High)
   - Creates incident in system
   - Generates unique Incident ID
   - Confirmation dialog appears

**After Submission:**
- Confirmation message with Incident ID
- Options to:
  - View the incident
  - Create another incident
  - Return to dashboard
  - Email report to stakeholders

### Auto-Save Feature

- Form auto-saves every 2 minutes
- "Last saved" timestamp displayed
- Draft recoverable if browser closes
- Drafts expire after 30 days

### Form Tips

- **Be Specific:** Include as much detail as possible
- **Use Clear Language:** Avoid jargon or abbreviations
- **Add Witnesses:** Include all potential witnesses
- **Attach Evidence:** Photos and documents strengthen reports
- **Timely Entry:** Enter incidents as soon as possible
- **Double Check:** Review all information before submitting

## Appendix

### Glossary

**AVL:** Automatic Vehicle Location - GPS tracking system
**CDL:** Commercial Driver's License
**Critical Incident:** Highest severity level requiring immediate action
**Hourmeter:** Device tracking engine operating hours
**Incident ID:** Unique identifier for each incident (e.g., INC-001)
**TYD AVL:** Tyler Technologies AVL integration
**VIN:** Vehicle Identification Number

### Status Definitions

**Incident Status:**
- **Open:** Newly reported, awaiting review
- **Under Review:** Being investigated
- **Resolved:** Actions completed, awaiting closure
- **Closed:** Fully resolved and archived

**Driver Status:**
- **Active:** Currently employed and driving
- **On Leave:** Temporarily not driving
- **Inactive:** No longer employed

**Vehicle Status:**
- **Active:** Currently in service
- **Maintenance:** Undergoing repairs
- **Inactive:** Out of service

**Communication Status:**
- **Pending Response:** Awaiting driver reply
- **Responded:** Driver has replied
- **Acknowledged:** Driver has confirmed receipt
- **Follow-up Required:** Additional action needed

### Severity Levels

**Critical:** Immediate attention required
- Serious injuries
- Major safety violations
- Police involvement
- Media attention

**High:** Urgent attention needed
- Minor injuries
- Significant behavior issues
- Safety concerns
- Multiple parties involved

**Medium:** Standard priority
- Behavioral incidents
- Minor violations
- No injuries
- Single incident

**Low:** Documentation purposes
- Minor issues
- Informational
- No action required
- Precautionary reports
